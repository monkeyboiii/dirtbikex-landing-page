## Your responsibility

When you ride, when you wrench, and when you trail-plan, you are responsible for your own safety. Specifically:

- **Wear appropriate protective gear** — helmet (current standard), goggles, jacket, gloves, knee braces, boots, neck brace as appropriate. The right gear for your discipline matters.
- **Ride within your ability.** Don't attempt features, jumps, speeds, or terrain that exceed your skill, fitness, or your bike's capabilities.
- **Train.** Take an MSF off-road class or equivalent. Learn from a qualified instructor before pushing your limits.
- **Maintain your bike** to manufacturer specifications and using qualified mechanics for work you aren't sure about. A search-result thread is not a torque-spec manual.
- **Follow the law.** Ride only where it is legal. Respect land use and OHV regulations. Carry the right registration and insurance.
- **Respect other people, land, and animals.** Don't cut trail, don't trespass, don't ride where prohibited.
- **Don't ride impaired.** Alcohol, drugs (recreational or prescription that affect reaction time), or extreme fatigue.
- **Have an emergency plan.** Ride with a buddy, carry communications, know where the nearest trauma center is.

## Content about modifications, tunes, and "shortcuts"

The forum will, inevitably, include posts about modifications, suspension changes, tuning, electrical work, fork swaps, jet kits, and similar topics. Some of these:

- **may void manufacturer warranty**;
- **may be illegal** on public roads or in certain jurisdictions (e.g., emissions controls, sound limits, lighting);
- **may compromise safety** if done incorrectly;
- **may not apply to your specific bike**, year, or trim;
- **may be flatly wrong** (this is the internet).

You are responsible for verifying any technical information with the manufacturer, a qualified mechanic, applicable laws, and your own testing. **Verify torque specs and electrical schematics against your bike's official service manual.**

## Content about trails and locations

- Trail conditions change. Closures, washouts, regrowth, hostile landowners, and other hazards may exist that aren't reflected in old posts.
- Always check the most current information from the land manager (BLM, USFS, state OHV agency, local club) before you ride a new area.
- Don't ride closed or restricted areas. We will not assist in defending users who post about illegal riding.

## In an emergency

This is a website. If someone is hurt, **call local emergency services first** (911 in the U.S., 112 in the EU/UK, 119 in parts of East Asia). After the situation is stable, you're welcome to share the story so others learn.

For suicide or mental-health crisis, contact a local crisis line. Do not rely on the forum.
