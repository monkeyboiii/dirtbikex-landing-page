This Privacy Policy describes how **DirtBikeX LLC** ("**DirtBikeX**," "**we**," "**us**," "**our**") collects, uses, shares, and protects personal information when you use the DirtBikeX website at dirtbikex.com, the community forum at forum.dirtbikex.com, and the DirtBikeX iOS application (together, the "**Services**").

This Policy applies globally. If you live in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, Switzerland, California, or another region with specific privacy rights, please see the [region-specific addenda](#region-specific-rights) below for additional information.

**Quick summary (not a substitute for the full policy):**

- We collect the information you give us (account details, posts), basic technical information (IP address, device data), and analytics about how you use the Services.
- We use it to operate the forum and app, keep them safe, and improve them.
- **We do not sell personal information.** We do not run third-party ad networks. We do not use your posts to train machine-learning models.
- You can request access, correction, deletion, or export of your data at **support@dirtbikex.com**.
- We are based in the United States and use cloud infrastructure in multiple regions, including outside the EEA. Specific transfer details are available on request from **support@dirtbikex.com**.

## 2. Information We Collect

We collect personal information in three ways: information you provide, information collected automatically, and information from third parties.

### 2.1 Information you provide

| Category | Examples | When collected |
|---|---|---|
| Account information | Email address, username, display name, profile picture, password (stored only as a one-way hash) | When you create an account |
| Profile information | Bike(s) you ride, riding location, bio, preferences | If you choose to add them |
| User Content | Posts, replies, comments, images, polls, direct messages within the forum | When you post |
| Communications | Emails, support requests, feedback | When you contact us |

### 2.2 Information collected automatically

| Category | Examples | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Log data | IP address, user-agent string, referrer, request paths, timestamps | Web/forum servers |
| Device data | Device model, OS version, app version, language, time zone, approximate location derived from IP | iOS app and web |
| Usage analytics | Pages and screens viewed, taps and clicks, session duration, feature interactions, crash reports | iOS app and web |
| Cookies and similar technologies | Session cookie, CSRF token, sign-in cookie, language preference | Web and forum |

We use **privacy-friendly, cookieless web analytics** on the marketing website (no cross-site tracking, no advertising identifiers). For details on cookies and similar technologies, see our [Cookie Policy](/cookies).

### 2.3 Information from third parties

- **Identity provider.** When you sign in, our third-party identity provider authenticates you and shares with us your email address, a unique user identifier, and (depending on the method you choose) display name and avatar.
- **Sign in with Apple / Google.** If you sign in with Apple or Google, those providers share with us your email (or a privacy relay address, in Apple's case) and a unique identifier.
- **Mobile platform.** If you enable push notifications, Apple provides us with a push-notification token associated with your device.

We do not buy personal data from data brokers, and we do not enrich your profile with third-party data sources.

## 4. How We Share Information

We share personal information only in the following circumstances:

- **With other users.** Your username, profile information, and posts are visible to other forum users (and, for public categories, to anyone on the internet). Direct messages are visible only to the participants and to system administrators where necessary for security or moderation.
- **With service providers (sub-processors).** We use third-party providers to operate the Services, including for cloud hosting, identity and authentication, email delivery, content delivery, error monitoring, and mobile-app crash and analytics services. These providers process personal information only on our instructions and under written data-protection agreements. A current list of sub-processors is available on request at **support@dirtbikex.com**. See Section 6 for general categories and locations.
- **With sponsors and advertisers.** We do **not** share your individual personal information with sponsors or advertisers. We may share aggregated, non-identifying statistics (e.g., "the splash screen was viewed 10,000 times last month").
- **For legal reasons.** We may disclose information when we believe in good faith that disclosure is required by law, legal process, or governmental request, or to protect the safety, rights, or property of any person.
- **In a business transfer.** If DirtBikeX is involved in a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, personal information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will provide notice before your personal information becomes subject to a different privacy policy.
- **With your consent.** For any other purpose disclosed to you at the time we collect the information.

**We do not sell personal information** for money, and we do not "share" personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising as those terms are defined under California law.

## 5. Cookies, Tracking, and Analytics

The marketing website uses minimal, privacy-friendly analytics that do not set tracking cookies. The forum uses session cookies that are strictly necessary for sign-in and security. The iOS app uses crash-reporting and analytics tools provided by a major mobile platform service to help us fix bugs and understand which features are used.

For a full list of cookies and similar technologies, see our [Cookie Policy](/cookies).

You can:

- block or delete cookies through your browser settings;

- opt out of marketing emails using the unsubscribe link in any email; and

## 6. International Data Transfers

DirtBikeX is based in the United States, and we use cloud infrastructure and service providers in multiple regions worldwide. As a result, your personal information may be transferred to, stored in, or processed in countries other than the one in which you reside, including countries outside the European Economic Area.

**Transfers from the EEA/UK/Switzerland.** When we transfer personal data outside the EEA, we rely on:

- **adequacy decisions** of the European Commission, where the destination country has been determined to provide an adequate level of protection; or
- **Standard Contractual Clauses** (the 2021 SCCs) and, where appropriate, supplementary technical and organizational measures.

**Transfers from other regions.** Where required by local law (for example, China's Personal Information Protection Law), we provide notice of cross-border transfers and seek your consent before transferring your personal information abroad. If you are unable to provide such consent, you may not be able to use the Services.

For details about the specific safeguards applicable to a given transfer, please contact **support@dirtbikex.com**.

## 7. How Long We Keep Information

We keep personal information only as long as we need it for the purposes described above. Specifically:

| Data | Retention |
|---|---|
| Account information | While your account is open. If you delete your account, see "Deletion and Anonymization" below. |
| User Content | While your account is open or the content remains posted on the forum. Posts may be retained after account deletion in anonymized form. |
| Server logs | Generally 30–90 days, longer if needed for security investigation |
| Crash reports | Generally 90 days |
| Email records | While the email account is active; deleted on a rolling basis after closure |
| Backups | Up to 90 days after deletion, in encrypted geo-redundant backups, after which deletion is propagated |
| Legal-hold records | As long as required by applicable law or legal process |

**Deletion and anonymization.** When you delete your account, your account is closed and your username is replaced with an anonymized identifier (for example, `former_user_a1b2c3`). Your User Content may remain visible to preserve the integrity of community discussions, but it will no longer be associated with you personally. If you wish to have specific posts removed at the time of deletion, you can request this in your deletion request. We will honor reasonable requests but reserve the right to retain content that is necessary for moderation, legal, or safety purposes.

## 8. Your Privacy Rights

You have the following rights with respect to your personal information. Depending on where you live, some of these are mandated by law (see [region-specific addenda](#region-specific-rights)); we extend the core rights globally as a matter of policy.

- **Access** — request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.
- **Correction** — ask us to fix inaccurate or incomplete information.
- **Deletion / erasure** — ask us to delete your personal information, subject to exceptions described in Section 7.
- **Portability** — request your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
- **Restriction** — ask us to limit how we use your information while we investigate a dispute.
- **Objection** — object to processing based on our legitimate interests, including for direct marketing.
- **Withdraw consent** — withdraw any consent you previously gave, without affecting prior lawful processing.
- **Lodge a complaint** — file a complaint with your local data-protection authority (see addenda below).
- **No retaliation** — we will not deny service, charge different prices, or provide a lesser quality of service because you exercise your rights.

**How to exercise your rights.** Email **support@dirtbikex.com** with the request. We may need to verify your identity (for example, by sending a confirmation to the email address on file). We will respond within 30 days, extendable by an additional 60 days if your request is complex.

**Authorized agents.** If you authorize someone to act on your behalf, we may require proof of authorization and verification of your identity.

## 9. Security

We take reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect personal information, including encryption in transit (TLS) and at rest, principle of least privilege for staff access, password hashing using modern algorithms, regular backups, and security monitoring. **No method of transmission or storage is perfectly secure**, and we cannot guarantee absolute security. If we become aware of a personal-data breach affecting you, we will notify you and the appropriate regulators as required by applicable law.

## <a id="region-specific-rights"></a>Region-Specific Addenda

### A. California Residents (CCPA / CPRA)

**Categories of personal information we collect and disclose.** In the past 12 months, we have collected the following categories of personal information (as defined by Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.140):

- *Identifiers* (e.g., name, email, IP address, account identifiers)
- *Customer records* (e.g., account information)
- *Internet or network activity* (e.g., browsing/usage data, log data)
- *Geolocation data* (approximate, derived from IP)
- *Audio/visual* (only if you upload images or audio yourself)
- *Inferences* drawn from the above (e.g., interests as a rider)

We disclose these categories to our service providers as described in Section 4. We disclose *Identifiers*, *Internet activity*, and *Inferences* in connection with operating the Services.

**We do not sell or share personal information** for cross-context behavioral advertising, as those terms are defined under the CCPA/CPRA. We have not done so in the preceding 12 months. We do not knowingly sell or share the personal information of consumers under 16.

**Sensitive personal information.** We do not use or disclose sensitive personal information for any purposes other than those permitted under Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.121(a) (e.g., to provide the services you request, security, short-term transient use).

**Your rights under the CCPA/CPRA.** You have the right to:

- know what personal information we have collected, used, disclosed, and the sources and purposes;
- delete your personal information, subject to legal exceptions;
- correct inaccurate personal information;
- opt out of sale or sharing (we do not sell or share, but you may submit a confirmatory opt-out);
- limit use of sensitive personal information (we do not use it beyond permitted purposes);
- non-discrimination for exercising your rights.

To exercise these rights, email **support@dirtbikex.com** with the subject "California Privacy Request." We will verify your identity using information you provide (typically the email address on file). You may also authorize an agent to act on your behalf; we will require proof of authorization.

If we deny your request, you have the right to appeal by replying to our denial.
