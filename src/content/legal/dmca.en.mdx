DirtBikeX respects the intellectual-property rights of others and expects users to do the same. This page describes how to report copyright infringement on the DirtBikeX Services and how counter-notices work. It is consistent with the U.S. Digital Millennium Copyright Act, 17 U.S.C. § 512 ("**DMCA**").

## Reporting copyright infringement

If you are a copyright owner (or an agent authorized to act for one) and you believe that material on DirtBikeX infringes your copyright, please send a written notice to our **DMCA Designated Agent**:

> **DMCA Designated Agent — DirtBikeX LLC**
> Copyright Manager
> 30 N Gould St Ste N, Sheridan, WY 82801, USA
> **Email:** support@dirtbikex.com
> **USCO Registration:** DMCA-1073161

Your notice **must** include all of the following to be effective under the DMCA:

1. A physical or electronic **signature** of the owner or authorized agent.
2. **Identification of the copyrighted work** claimed to have been infringed (or a representative list, if multiple works at a single site).
3. **Identification of the material** that is claimed to be infringing, with enough detail for us to locate it. Direct URLs to the post(s) are best.
4. **Your contact information** — address, phone number, and email.
5. A statement that you have a **good-faith belief** that use of the material is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.
6. A statement, made **under penalty of perjury**, that the information in the notice is accurate and that you are the owner or authorized to act for the owner of an exclusive right that is allegedly infringed.

> ⚠️ **Materially false notices have consequences.** Under 17 U.S.C. § 512(f), any person who knowingly materially misrepresents that material is infringing may be liable for damages, including costs and attorneys' fees. Filing a frivolous DMCA notice can also lead to suspension of your account.

We will:

- promptly disable access to material identified in a valid notice;
- forward a copy of the notice to the user who posted it;
- terminate, in appropriate circumstances, the accounts of users who are **repeat infringers**.

## Counter-notice

If you are a user whose material has been removed under the DMCA and you believe the removal was a mistake or that the material is non-infringing (for example, you have a license, or your use is fair use), you may send a **counter-notice** to the DMCA agent above.

Your counter-notice **must** include:

1. Your **physical or electronic signature**.
2. **Identification of the material** that was removed, and its location before removal.
3. A statement, **under penalty of perjury**, that you have a good-faith belief the material was removed as a result of mistake or misidentification.
4. Your **name, address, and telephone number**.
5. A statement that you **consent to the jurisdiction** of the federal district court for the judicial district in which your address is located (or, if outside the U.S., for any judicial district in which DirtBikeX may be found) and that you will **accept service of process** from the person who submitted the original notice or their agent.

If we receive a valid counter-notice, we will forward it to the original complainant and inform them that we will restore the material in 10 to 14 business days unless they file a court action seeking a restraining order against the user.

## Repeat-infringer policy

We will, in appropriate circumstances, terminate accounts of users who are **repeat infringers**. We may also terminate accounts for a single severe infringement at our discretion.

## What's not covered by this policy

- **Trademark complaints** — please email **support@dirtbikex.com** with "Trademark complaint" in the subject line and describe the issue.
- **Privacy / personal-information complaints** — see our [Privacy Policy](/privacy) and email **support@dirtbikex.com**.
- **Harassment, hate speech, or community-rule violations** — see our [Community Guidelines](/community-guidelines) and use the in-forum Flag button or email **support@dirtbikex.com**.
- **Non-U.S. copyright systems (notice-and-action under EU law, etc.).** We honor takedown requests under the EU Digital Services Act and equivalent laws in other jurisdictions through the same email channel above; please clearly state the legal basis of your request.

## EU users: Article 16 DSA notice-and-action

If you are submitting a notice under the European Union Digital Services Act (Regulation (EU) 2022/2065), please include:

- the **specific reason** the content is illegal under EU or member-state law;
- the **exact URL(s)** of the content;
- your **name and email** (unless the notice concerns a criminal offense relating to children);
- a **declaration of good faith** that the notice is accurate and complete.

We will confirm receipt and decide promptly. We provide a statement of reasons for any moderation decision affecting EU recipients, in accordance with Article 17 DSA.
